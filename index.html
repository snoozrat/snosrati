About

I am a 2nd year PhD student in the Cognitive and Information Sciences Department at the University of California Merced. My work focuses on understanding how we make sense of abstract concepts, especially space and time.

My broader interests include the interplay of culture, language, and individual differences in shaping our conceptual systems. In my free time, I enjoy taking pictures, watching old samurai movies, and making entirely too much pasta.

Research

My research explores how people represent and reason about abstract domains such as space and time. I study how cognitive processes and linguistic features influence our understanding of these fundamental concepts. Feel free to contact me if you’re interested in potential collaborations or want to know more about my ongoing projects.
